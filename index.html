About

Operator with one foot in product and one foot in research.

I build and explain technical systems where markets, AI, and infrastructure overlap. That has meant shipping developer-facing products, architecting data platforms, leading technical onboarding, and translating complex systems into something founders, researchers, and users can actually act on.

Today I’m the cofounder of Internet Motion, where I’m building prediction-market infrastructure and developer tooling. Before that, I advised deep-tech startups on developer-first GTM strategy, ecosystem activation, and early hiring. Earlier in my career, I worked on business development and network growth at Celestia Labs, invested across venture and hedge funds at Galaxy Digital, and did labor-market research with Brookings and the U.S. House of Representatives.

My background is unusually split between technical systems and institutional environments. I studied applied mathematics and economics at Columbia, later completed the Schwarzman Scholars master’s program in global affairs at Tsinghua, and have spent the past several years working on products, markets, and policy questions that sit upstream of how technology actually gets adopted.
